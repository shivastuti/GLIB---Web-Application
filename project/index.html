About Me

Quote ‘Efficiency and Perfection is the art of living’ Unquote-This statement rules every aspect of my life. It is not being ambitious or having goals that drives me, but what really keeps me going and craving for more knowledge is the inquisition “What next?” i.e. after a specific target is achieved, what more can be learnt from the surroundings and what can be the next goal. It is this need to know more that coaxed me to participate in a variety of curricular and co-curricular activities throughout my school life and college life and even in being a part of many initiatives at work as well. I don’t believe that anything can be perfect. It might sound contrary to the first statement of this essay but if we deem something perfect we neglect the scope of improvement which is imperative to actually get closer to achieving something impeccable. Thus, I believe that we must aim for perfection, because if we aim high, we would end up reaching pretty close to it, if not achieving ‘it’ absolutely. I belong to a small family comprising of my parents, grandmother and a younger brother besides me. My father is a Government official and my mother is a high school teacher. My parents have been a constant source of inspiration to me. They have always encouraged both their kids to question what already is and to be creative in thoughts. I believe a very important part of what actually makes me the strong, forthright and passionate person that I am is the sense of independence that they imbued in us since childhood. The independence to give vent to my thoughts, the freedom to express in written, vocal and other artistic ways and the strongly imbibed belief that imagination has no bounds, hence forms the core layer of my persona. I have learnt from them the fact about being the change that you want to see since they are actively involved in various social upliftment programs. This inspired me to be a part of various social causes like the education of underprivileged children, spreading awareness about blood donation, working for the Wildlife society at the school. I had been a member of the school student council and the President of the YSM (Young Students Movement) that conducted these social awareness and upliftment programs in the city on behalf of my school. I believe “To be is not the question, it is to be your real self”. It is important to be aware of ourselves and for such realization knowledge is the only key. Thus in my pursuit of knowledge, I aspire to discover my real self.
